Sai Ramya Gajula "acts in a play"

Play: An Enemy of the People Act 3

Author: Henrik Ibsen


Hovstad's spoken text.
Hovstad.
By all means, Mr. Mayor. I am at your service. But let me relieve you of your—
(takes STOCKMANN's hat and stick and puts them on a chair).
Won't you sit down?



Peter Stockmann's spoken text.
Peter Stockmann.
(sitting down by the table).
Thank you.
(HOVSTAD sits down.)
I have had an extremely annoying experience to-day, Mr. Hovstad.

    

Hovstad's spoken text.
Hovstad.
Really? Ah well, I expect with all the various business you have to attend to—



Peter Stockmann's spoken text.
Peter Stockmann.
The Medical Officer of the Baths is responsible for what happened today.



Hovstad's spoken text.
Hovstad.
Indeed? The Doctor?



Peter Stockmann's spoken text.
Peter Stockmann.
He has addressed a kind of report to the Baths Committee on the subject of certain supposed defects in the Baths.



Hovstad's spoken text.
Hovstad.
Has he indeed?



Peter Stockmann's spoken text.
Peter Stockmann.
Yes—has he not told you? I thought he said—



Hovstad's spoken text.
Hovstad.
Ah, yes—it is true he did mention something about—



Aslaksen's spoken text.
Aslaksen
(coming from the printing-room).
I ought to have that copy.



Hovstad's spoken text.
Hovstad
(angrily).
Ahem!—there it is on the desk.



Aslaksen's spoken text.
Aslaksen
(taking it).
Right.



Peter Stockmann's spoken text.
Peter Stockmann.
But look there—that is the thing I was speaking of!



Aslaksen's spoken text.
Aslaksen.
Yes, that is the Doctor's article, Mr. Mayor.
